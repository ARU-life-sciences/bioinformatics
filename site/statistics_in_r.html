Statistics in R

Statistics are a critical part of the bioinformaticians toolkit, and indeed across biological sciences. Here, we introduce a few key statistical concepts, and demonstrate how to perform them in R. These concepts are based around the types of data you have, for example categorical or numeric, or combinations of both.

Contents:

  • Descriptive statistics
  • Standard errors and confidence intervals
  • Chi Square test
  • T-test, ANOVA
  • Correlation and regression
  • Generalised linear models

Descriptive statistics

Data exploration is a key part of any statistical analysis. Descriptive statistics can be used to explore the distribution of your data, and to provide a summary of the key features of your data. This can be particularly useful when you have a large dataset, where it is not possible to view all the data at once. Here we will demonstrate how to calculate and visualise descriptive statistics in R.

Descriptive statistics tutorial

Standard error and condfidence intervals

In statistics we have this idea of uncertainty. We often want to know how certain we are of our estimates. For example, if we have a sample mean, we might want to know how certain we are that this mean represents the true population mean. This is where the standard error comes in. The standard error is a measure of the uncertainty of our estimates. In this section we will demonstrate how to calculate the standard error and the related quantity, confidence intervals in R.

If all our data is categorical, then we can use the chi-square test to determine if there is a significant association between the variables. Here we will demonstrate how to calculate and visualise the chi-square test in R.

T-test

The t-test is a statistical test used to determine if there is a significant difference between the means of two groups. The t-test is commonly used to compare the mean of a sample to a known value, and to compare the means of two samples. Here we will demonstrate how to calculate and visualise the t-test in R.

T-test tutorial

Correlation and regression

Two related concepts in statistics are correlation and regression. Correlation is a measure of the strength of the relationship between two variables. Regression is a statistical method used to model the relationship between two numerical variables. Here we will demonstrate how to calculate and visualise correlation and regression in R.
